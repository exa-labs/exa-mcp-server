# Build Your Marketing Plan

## Tool Usage

Use `web_search_exa` to find high-performing content in the user's niche, discover what topics resonate, and identify thought leaders to learn from.

## Core Principle

**Marketing is sales at scale.** Don't confuse marketing with advertising. Marketing is about making fans, not headlines. Start by spending time, not money. Blog posts are free. Twitter, Instagram, YouTube are free. Only spend money after you know exactly who you're reaching.

## Prerequisites

Before marketing, you should have:
- A community you belong to
- A product people are paying for
- ~100 customers (repeat customers = product-market fit)
- Experience selling one-on-one

## The Three Levels of Content

### Level 1: Educate
- Share what you've learned from building your business
- 100 customers = 100 things worth sharing
- Doesn't need to be polished — just consistent
- Search `"[niche] tips" OR "[niche] lessons learned"` to find what educational content performs well

### Level 2: Inspire
- Go beyond teaching into motivating
- Share your journey, struggles, successes
- Be vulnerable and authentic — like Gimlet's *StartUp* podcast
- Search `"[niche] founder story" OR "building [product type] in public"` to find inspiring examples

### Level 3: Entertain
- Hardest but most far-reaching
- Entertainment is the king of content on every platform

## Social Media Guide

- **Two accounts**: Personal (you) and business (the company)
- **Be authentic.** Focus on ideas, not self-promotion.
- **Build in public.** Share what you're working on, improving, learning.
- **Pick one platform** rather than juggling all of them.

Research what works:
- **Find top creators**: Search `"[niche] newsletter" OR "[niche] blog" OR "[niche] podcast"` to find thought leaders to study.
- **Find what resonates**: Search `"[niche] content strategy" OR "[niche] audience building"` for proven approaches.

## Email: Own Your Audience

- Social media = rented land (algorithms change, accounts get shut down)
- Email = owned land (direct line to customers, no algorithm)
- Start building an email list immediately
- Offer something valuable in exchange (guide, PDF, checklist)

## Spend Money Last

- Most growth you see is paid for. Don't be fooled.
- Only spend on ads after organic traction and known customer profile
- Spend money on customers (rewards, loyalty) before acquisition

## Output

1. Primary content platform and posting schedule
2. 5 content ideas for each level (educate, inspire, entertain) — informed by real trending topics from Exa
3. Email list strategy (what to offer, how to collect)
4. "Build in public" plan
5. 3 thought leaders in their niche to study (found via Exa)
