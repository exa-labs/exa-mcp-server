# Get Your First Customers

## Tool Usage

Use `web_search_exa` to find real potential customers, discover where they gather, and research outreach strategies. Use `linkedin_search_exa` to find specific people matching the ideal customer profile.

## Core Principle

**Skip the launch. Focus on selling.** "Viral success" is a myth. Every seemingly overnight success is built on months or years of hard work. Sell one by one, learn from each interaction, build momentum.

## The Concentric Circles of Sales

### Circle 1: Friends and Family
- Start here. Yes, it's uncomfortable. Do it anyway.
- Pitch them on being your first customers, not investors
- Ask for honest feedback, not social media posts

### Circle 2: Your Community
- The community you identified and have been contributing to
- Three steps:
  1. **Make a list of everyone** who has written about a similar business — search `"[product type] review" OR "[product type] experience"` to find people in this space
  2. **Contact them all personally** — walk them through your product, do it hundreds of times
  3. **Ask for candid feedback** — not reviews, just honest feedback

### Circle 3: Strangers (Cold Outreach)
- Cold emails, calls, messages — this is how Gumroad grew
- Sahil scoured the web for people who could benefit and emailed them personally, thousands of times
- **Use Exa to do what Sahil did at scale**: Search `"[problem] using [current workaround]"` to find people who need your solution. Use `linkedin_search_exa` to find professionals matching your ideal customer profile.
- Example cold email:
  > "Hi John, I saw you're selling a PDF on your website using PayPal, and manually emailing everyone who buys the PDF. I built a service called Gumroad which basically automates all of this. I'd love to show it to you. Best, Sahil"
- Don't copy/paste. Each email refines your ability to write better ones.

## Pricing

- Massive difference between free and $1 (the "zero price effect")
- **Cost-based**: Your costs + a margin (20-50%)
- **Value-based**: What it's worth to the customer
- Start low, raise prices as product improves

## Key Metrics

- Manual sales = 99% of early growth. Word of mouth = 99% of later growth.
- You need far fewer customers than you think. Slack's IPO: 575 customers = 40% of revenue.
- **Product-market fit** = repeat customers who sign up and use your product on their own

## When to "Launch"

Don't launch until 100 paying customers. Then launch as a celebration of your community's support.

## Output

1. List of 10 friends/family to pitch this week
2. List of 10 community members to reach out to (found via Exa)
3. Cold outreach template with specific details sourced from Exa research
4. Initial pricing strategy
5. Weekly sales goal and tracking method
