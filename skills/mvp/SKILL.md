# Build Your MVP

## Tool Usage

Use `web_search_exa` to find real MVP examples, discover no-code tools, and research what similar products look like today.

## Core Principle

**Build as little as possible.** Start delivering value to your community as quickly as possible. Not beautiful, polished, or complete.

## The Three Stages

### Stage 1: Manual
- Solve the problem by hand for each customer
- You are the product — customer service, fulfillment, and engineering
- Write down every step — this becomes your process

### Stage 2: Processized
- Document your process so anyone could follow it
- If you go on vacation, someone else can take over
- This is your "magic piece of paper"

### Stage 3: Productized
- Automate each task so customers can use it without you
- Only build what you've already proven works manually

## Four Build Questions

1. **Can I ship it in a weekend?** If not, reduce scope.
2. **Is it making customers' lives better?** That's the bar.
3. **Will a customer pay for it?** Profitable from day one.
4. **Can I get feedback quickly?** Build for people who can tell you if it's working.

## What to Build

Most apps are just forms and lists (CRUD). Your MVP should be no more complex.

- **One thing.** Your product does one thing, at first.
- **No polish.** CraigsList has never been pretty.
- **Charge money.** Huge difference between free and $1 (zero price effect).
- **Use existing tools.** Carrd, Gumroad, Stripe, Airtable, Google Forms, Zapier, Notion.

Research the best tools:
- **Find the right stack**: Search `"best no-code tools for [product type]"` or `"how to build [product type] without code"`.
- **Learn from similar MVPs**: Search `"[similar product] MVP story" OR "[similar product] how it started"`.
- **Find building blocks**: Search `"[product type] template" OR "[product type] boilerplate"`.

## What NOT to Build

- Features you think you'll need "someday"
- For scale — you don't have scale problems yet
- A mobile app when a website works
- Code when a spreadsheet works

## Essentials Checklist

- [ ] Name your business (two real words > made-up word; pass the "radio test")
- [ ] Buy a domain (~$10/year)
- [ ] Build a simple website (Carrd, Gumroad, or similar)
- [ ] Set up payments (Stripe or Square)
- [ ] Create an email for customer communication
