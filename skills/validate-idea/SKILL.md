# Validate Your Idea

## Tool Usage

Use `web_search_exa` to research competitors, find real discussions about the problem, and validate demand with actual market data.

## Core Principle

**Validation happens through selling, not building.** Most founders spend months building a product nobody wants. Validate by selling a manual version first.

## Step 1: Define the Problem

Ask the user:
- Who specifically has this problem? (Not "businesses" but "freelance graphic designers who struggle with invoicing")
- How are they solving it today? (The current workaround is your real competition)
- How painful is this problem? (Mild annoyance vs. hair-on-fire)
- Would they pay to make it go away?

Then research:
- **Find existing solutions**: Search `"[problem] tools"` or `"best [solution type] for [audience]"` to discover competitors.
- **Read real complaints**: Search `"[problem] frustrating" OR "[problem] wish there was"` scoped to forums to see if people are vocal about this pain.
- **Check failed attempts**: Search `"[product type] startup shut down" OR "[product type] post-mortem"` to learn from others.

## Step 2: Can You Solve It Manually First?

- Create a manual valuable process ("processizing")
- Do it yourself first. Write down every step
- If you can solve it manually for a few people, you can eventually automate it

## Step 3: Will People Pay?

The ultimate validation is a transaction:
- Can you charge for this manual service right now?
- Have you talked to at least 10 potential customers?
- Have at least 3 said they'd pay (or actually paid)?

Research willingness to pay:
- **Find pricing signals**: Search `"[similar product] pricing"` to see what people currently pay.
- **Find buying discussions**: Search `"[product type] worth paying for" OR "[product type] recommendation"` to gauge spending sentiment.

## Step 4: Four Questions Before Building

1. **Can I ship it in a weekend?** First iteration should be prototyped in 2-3 days.
2. **Is it making customers' lives better?** That's a minimum viable product.
3. **Is a customer willing to pay?** Profitable from day one.
4. **Can I get feedback quickly?** Faster feedback = faster improvement.

## Red Flags

- Nobody currently trying to solve this problem
- Can't name 10 specific people who have it
- Only validation is "my friends think it's cool"
- You need to educate people that they have this problem

## Green Flags

- People already paying for inferior solutions
- You've manually solved this for a few people
- Community actively complaining about this problem
- You can describe customer and pain point in one sentence

## Output

Clear verdict:
- **Validated**: Strong signals, proceed to MVP — include competitors found and where the idea differs
- **Needs more validation**: Specific next steps — include real communities and discussions to monitor
- **Pivot**: Fundamental changes needed — suggest directions based on actual market gaps found
