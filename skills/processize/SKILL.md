# Processize Before You Productize

## Tool Usage

Use `web_search_exa` to find examples of manual-first businesses, discover how similar products started before software, and locate communities where future customers gather.

## Core Principle

**Processize before you productize.** Every great product started as someone doing the work by hand. Gumroad started with Sahil collecting PayPal emails and sending payments to creators one by one.

## Step 1: What's the Product Idea?

Ask the user to describe what they want to build. Then strip it down:
- What is the **one thing** this product does for a customer?
- What does the customer have before and after?
- If you had to deliver this value with zero technology — just you, a phone, and a spreadsheet — how would you do it?

## Step 2: Who Needs This Today?

Connect the idea to a real community:
- Who is already trying to solve this with a workaround?
- Where do these people hang out?
- Can you name 10 specific people who have this problem?

Research the landscape:
- **Find people with this problem**: Search `"[problem] workaround" OR "[problem] how I currently handle"` to find real people describing manual solutions.
- **Find where they gather**: Search `"[audience] community" OR "[audience] forum"` to locate reachable communities.

If you can't name 10 people, go back to `/find-community`.

## Step 3: Design the Manual Version

Walk through how to deliver value by hand:
- **What does the customer give you?** (An email, a file, a description)
- **What do you do with it?** (Every step, in order)
- **What do you give back?** (The deliverable)
- **How long does it take?** (Per customer)

Be specific. "I process their request" is not a step.

## Step 4: Write the Magic Piece of Paper

Document your manual process so clearly that someone else could do it:
1. **Trigger** — What kicks off the process?
2. **Steps** — Numbered list of exactly what to do
3. **Tools needed** — What you use at each step
4. **Time per customer** — How long each step takes
5. **Handoff** — How you deliver the result

## Step 5: Charge for It

> "There is a massive difference between free and $1."

- Set a price based on time and value to the customer
- Reach out to 3 people from your community this week
- Deliver the manual version and collect payment

## Step 6: When to Productize

Only automate what you've proven works manually:
- 10+ paying customers delivered to
- Can predict exactly what they'll ask for
- Steps haven't changed in weeks
- More time on delivery than finding customers

Then automate one step at a time, starting with whatever takes the most time per customer.

## Output

1. One-sentence description of what the product does manually
2. The magic piece of paper — full manual process, step by step
3. 3 specific people or communities to deliver it to this week (found via Exa)
4. Initial price point
5. The first step to automate when ready (not now)
