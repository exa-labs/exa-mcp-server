# Grow Sustainably

## Tool Usage

Use `web_search_exa` to research SaaS tools that replace hiring, find industry benchmarks, and discover how similar companies grew sustainably. Use `company_research_exa` to research specific companies the user mentions.

## Core Principle

**Profitability is a superpower.** It gives you infinite runway, clarity, and control. Spend less than you make. When you're profitable, you can take your time, make the right decisions, and move at your own pace.

## Don't Spend Money You Don't Have

**Profit = Revenue - Costs**

### Two Types of Costs
- **Variable (COGS)**: Scale with each unit — payment processing, hosting, fraud prevention
- **Fixed**: Don't scale with revenue — domain, hosting, people. #1 fixed cost is always people.

### Cost-Cutting Rules

1. **Pay yourself as little as possible, at least to start.** Sahil started at $36K/year in SF.
2. **Hire software, not humans.** Search `"best tools to automate [function]"` to find software alternatives before committing to a hire.
3. **Don't get an office.** Remote is the default. Get one later as a reward.
4. **Don't move to Silicon Valley.** Lower costs = faster path to profitability.
5. **Outsource everything.** You and your robots first. Then freelancers. Then employees.

## Growth Mindset

- You don't need to dominate the market or disrupt anything
- The longest-lived businesses are some of the smallest: restaurants, hotels, family firms
- Your company grows as quickly as your customers determine
- Working more hours doesn't necessarily mean faster growth

## Fundraising (If You Must)

- **Bootstrap first.** Profitability gives you leverage.
- **Consider Regulation Crowdfunding.** Turn customers into investors.
- **Find alternatives**: Search `"bootstrapper-friendly funding" OR "revenue-based financing for startups"` for current alternatives to traditional VC.
- **If you take VC:** profitability means lower dilution and retained control.

## Avoiding Burnout

Two fatal mistakes:
1. **Running out of money** — solved by the above
2. **Running out of energy** — equally dangerous

- Approach co-founder relationships like a marriage. Discuss success, exit, growth speed.
- Don't treat it as all-or-nothing. Lots of real estate between "beach lifestyle" and "working 24/7."
- Hire when it hurts — that means a mature business for new people to fit into.

## Output

For any business decision, evaluate:
1. Impact on profitability (revenue and cost implications)
2. Reversibility (avoid irreversible decisions like long-term leases)
3. Whether it's driven by customer needs or ego/vanity
4. Whether there's a cheaper/simpler alternative (found via Exa)
5. The "default alive or default dead" test
