# Find Your Community

## Tool Usage

Use `web_search_exa` throughout. Ground every recommendation in real search results — real community names, real links, real pain points.

## Core Principle

**Start with community, not with a product idea.** The best minimalist businesses are built by people already embedded in a community who notice a problem worth solving.

## Framework

Walk the user through:

1. **What communities are you already part of?** Professional groups, hobby communities, online forums, local organizations, identity-based groups, alumni networks.
2. **Where do you spend time online?** Reddit, Discord, Slack groups, Twitter/X, forums, Facebook groups, Substacks.
3. **What problems do you hear people complain about repeatedly?** Persistent, recurring pain points within communities you understand deeply.
4. **Which of these communities would you excite you to serve for years?**

## Research Their Communities

Once the user shares interests, do real research:

- **Find active communities**: Search `"[interest] community forum Discord Slack group"` to discover where people gather.
- **Surface pain points**: Search `"[community] biggest problems frustrations"` or `"[community] wish there was a tool for"` to find unmet needs.
- **Gauge size**: Search `"[community name] members active users"` to estimate if it's large enough.
- **Find adjacent niches**: Search `"communities like [community name]"` or `"[interest] subreddit alternatives"`.

Present concrete options — real community names, real links, real pain points people are posting about.

## Evaluation Criteria

For each community, evaluate:
- **Are you a genuine member?** You should understand the language, values, culture.
- **Is the problem painful enough to pay for?** Not every problem is a business.
- **Can you reach these people?** Do you know where they gather?
- **Is it large enough but not too large?** Niche you can dominate, not so broad you disappear.

## Anti-patterns

- Inventing a community from scratch rather than joining an existing one
- Choosing purely for market size rather than genuine interest
- Skipping participation and jumping to "what can I sell"
- Targeting too broad an audience

## Output

Narrow down to 1-3 communities with specific problems identified:
- The community
- The persistent problem
- How the user is connected
- Where the community gathers (with real links from Exa)
