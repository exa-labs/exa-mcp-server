# Define Your Company Values

## Tool Usage

Use `web_search_exa` to find examples of company values from admired companies in the user's industry, and research how similar companies build culture.

## Core Principle

**Focus on culture before hiring.** Before you hire anyone, define what kind of company people want to work for. Values are not generic two-word commandments — they're for stating the non-obvious, in non-obvious ways.

## Why Values Matter

- Tell employees how to behave every day AND in extreme situations
- More efficient than 1,000-page manuals — good values stick in the brain
- Attract the right people and repel the wrong ones — both are valuable
- Allow the company to scale beyond your personal involvement

## Gumroad's Values (As Starting Points)

1. **Judged by the Work** — What matters is the experience creators and customers have
2. **Seek Superlinearities** — Every day producing superlinear returns on your time investment
3. **Everyone is a CEO** — You are the CEO of your function, responsible for its execution
4. **Dare to Be Open** — Complete information symmetry, no meetings, no secrets

## How to Create Your Own Values

Walk through:

1. **What do you believe that most people don't?** Values should be non-obvious, sometimes polarizing.
2. **How should people behave when no one is watching?** Values are for moments without a manager.
3. **What would you fire someone for, even if they're performing well?** That reveals true values.
4. **What would you celebrate, even if it didn't help the bottom line?** That's also a value.
5. **Write them as stories, not slogans.** "Focus on the user" is a slogan. Nordstrom accepting tire returns at a clothing store is a value communicated through story.

Research values from companies the user admires:
- **Find real examples**: Search `"[admired company] company values" OR "[admired company] culture"`.
- **Find industry-specific values**: Search `"[industry] startup values" OR "[industry] company culture examples"`.

## Operationalizing Values

- Communicate publicly — job posts, website, onboarding
- Use in feedback: "This aligns with our value of X" or "This doesn't reflect our value of Y"
- Revisit regularly — values evolve as your company grows

## Output

1. 3-5 company values with descriptions and example stories
2. How each value shows up in hiring decisions
3. How each value shows up in day-to-day work
4. Anti-patterns for each value (what it does NOT mean)
